The easy method

Start with sauce, then build the bowl.

Instant ramen is fast. NoodleBomb makes it feel finished. Add the sauce to hot broth or hot water, stir until the bowl tastes balanced, then bring in texture: egg, scallions, vegetables, crispy onions, leftover chicken, tofu, or whatever is already in the fridge.

Use Original for the most flexible bowl, Spicy Tokyo when you want heat, and Citrus Shoyu when you want a brighter finish. If you are not sure which one fits your pantry, the Trio gives you all three Pour flavors for $29.99.

Five fast upgrades

Better ramen without making dinner a project.

01
Original

Egg, scallions, and Original

Cook the noodles, stir Original into the hot base, then top with a soft egg and scallions. This is the everyday move when you want bold flavor without extra heat.

02
Spicy Tokyo

Chili-garlic heat bowl

Use Spicy Tokyo when the bowl needs heat. Add cooked noodles, a little hot noodle water, cabbage or greens, and sesame for a fast spicy noodle bowl.

03
Citrus Shoyu

Bright vegetable ramen

Citrus Shoyu works well with vegetables, mushrooms, and a lighter broth. It gives instant ramen a cleaner finish without turning the bowl heavy.

04
Cold noodles

Lunch bowl from leftovers

Rinse cooked noodles cold, toss with NoodleBomb, then add cucumber, egg, chicken, tofu, or snap peas. It travels better than a hot bowl.

05
Rice bowl

Use the toppings twice

If you already chopped toppings for ramen, use the rest over rice the next day. Add NoodleBomb, heat, and one crunchy thing.

FAQ

Instant ramen upgrade questions

01

How much sauce should I use?

Start with 1 tablespoon for one bowl. Taste, then add more if you want a louder bowl.

02

Do I need broth?

No. You can stir sauce into broth, hot water, or toss it directly with noodles and a splash of hot noodle water.

03

Which flavor should I start with?

Original is the most flexible, Spicy Tokyo brings heat, and Citrus Shoyu brings a brighter finish.
