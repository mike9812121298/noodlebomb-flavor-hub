Getting it to you.

The product

What’s in the bottle.

What ingredients are in NoodleBomb?+

Real, food-first ingredients: fermented soy sauce, water, sugar, garlic, sesame oil, kombu, mirin, monosodium glutamate, salt, and a flavor-specific spice profile. No high-fructose corn syrup. No artificial colors. Specific ingredients vary by flavor — the full panel is on the back of every bottle.

Contains: wheat (in the soy sauce base), soy.

Does NoodleBomb contain wheat or gluten?+

Yes. NoodleBomb contains wheat in the fermented soy sauce base. It is not safe for people with celiac disease or wheat allergies. We’d rather be honest about what’s in the bottle than carry a label we haven’t earned.

Does it contain MSG?+

Yes. NoodleBomb contains monosodium glutamate. MSG is the same compound naturally present in tomatoes, parmesan, mushrooms, and the dashi at the heart of Japanese cooking. It’s what makes savory taste savory. We use it because it earns its place in the recipe.

How long does a bottle last?+

A 7 fl oz bottle is roughly 14 servings. Most people get 3–4 weeks out of one bottle.

Does NoodleBomb need refrigeration?+

Unopened: shelf-stable for 18 months. Refrigerate after opening and use within 6 months for peak flavor.

Heat & pairing

Which one’s for me.

How spicy is Spicy Tokyo?+

Medium heat — about a 5 out of 10. Noticeable warmth, layered over deep umami, not punishment-level spice. If you love sambal but find ghost-pepper sauce silly, you’re in the right zone.

Where do I start if I’ve never tried it?+

Original — the foundation. Garlic, sesame, smooth soy. Most versatile.

Spicy Tokyo — if you reach for chili oil first.

Citrus Shoyu — if you like brightness and clean finishes.

Or grab the Trio and skip the decision.

What can I put it on besides ramen?+

Rice bowls, dumplings, stir-fry, eggs, roasted vegetables, wings, marinades, dressings. If it’s savory, it probably works. See the recipes →
