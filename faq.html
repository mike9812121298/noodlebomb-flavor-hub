Where are NoodleBomb sauces made?+

Bonney Lake, Washington. Every bottle is mixed, filled, and labeled by hand in our kitchen. Small-batch, family-owned, made in the USA.

What ingredients are in NoodleBomb?+

Fermented soy sauce, water, sugar, garlic, sesame oil, kombu, mirin, monosodium glutamate, salt, and a flavor-specific spice profile. Contains: wheat (in the soy sauce base), soy. Specific ingredients vary by flavor — the full panel is on the back of every bottle.

How spicy is Spicy Tokyo?+

Medium-high heat — flavor-forward, not pain-forward. Roasted chili layered over deep umami. If you reach for sambal first and find ghost-pepper sauce silly, you’re in the right zone.

Can I use NoodleBomb on things other than ramen?+

Yes — that’s the whole point. Rice bowls, dumplings, sushi, stir-fry, eggs, roasted vegetables, glazed proteins, marinades, wings, dips. If it’s savory, it probably works.

How long does an opened bottle last?+

Refrigerated, about 6 months for peak flavor. Unopened, the bottle is shelf-stable for 18 months. A 7 fl oz bottle is roughly 14 servings — most people get 3 to 4 weeks of regular use.

What’s the difference between the three sauces?+

Original — turn up the flavor, not the heat. Garlic, sesame, smooth soy. Spicy Tokyo — bold heat, deep umami. Dark soy, roasted chili. Citrus Shoyu — bright lift, bold finish. Citrus over a clean shoyu base.

Does NoodleBomb contain wheat or gluten?+

Yes. NoodleBomb contains wheat (in the fermented soy sauce base) and soy. It is not safe for celiac disease or wheat allergies. Allergen panel: contains wheat, contains soy, contains MSG.

Is NoodleBomb vegan?+

Check the back label of each bottle for the current ingredient list. The base is plant-derived (soy, garlic, sesame, kombu, mirin) and we don’t use animal-derived ingredients in the flavors we currently ship, but we don’t carry a vegan certification — the label is the source of truth.

How should I store NoodleBomb?+

Cool, dry place before opening. Refrigerate after opening and use within 6 months for peak flavor. Don’t leave it on the stovetop — heat dulls the top notes.

What size is the bottle?+

7 fl oz, with a drizzle cap built for control. About 14 servings per bottle.

What’s MSG and why is it in the sauce?+

MSG (monosodium glutamate) is a glutamate that delivers umami. It’s the same compound naturally present in seaweed, tomatoes, parmesan, mushrooms, and the dashi at the heart of Japanese cooking. We use it because it earns its place in the recipe. Allergen panel discloses it on every bottle.
