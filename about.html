About · NoodleBomb

Before NoodleBomb,
ramen sauce didn’t exist.

Nobody made it. We did. Three flavors, hand-bottled in small batches in Bonney Lake, Washington. No fillers. No compromise. Pour bold.

The story

Built in the kitchen,
not in a lab.

Ashley March started cooking ramen the way her grandmother did — broth that took a day, tare that took weeks. Restaurant ramen, made at home. The bottleneck was always the sauce: the dark, layered, glossy concentrate that turns a bowl of plain noodles into something you remember.

Most jars on the shelf were sweet, watery, or full of fillers. So she made her own. Forty test batches. Three made the cut.

Original is the foundation — garlic and sesame, smooth soy, the one you reach for first. Spicy Tokyo is dark soy and roasted chili over deep umami. Citrus Shoyu is the bright lift that cuts through a heavy bowl. Together they’re a kit, not a flavor of the month.

What we stand for

Four things,
non-negotiable.

01

Small-batch, by hand

Every bottle is mixed, filled, and labeled in our Bonney Lake kitchen. Not licensed. Not contracted. Made.

02

Real ingredients only

Fermented soy, real garlic, toasted sesame, kombu, mirin. No corn-syrup base. No flavor enhancers shipped in barrels.

03

Three flavors, kept

We rejected forty. The three we shipped earned their slot. We’d rather be narrow and right than wide and forgettable.

04

Premium without theater

$11.99 a bottle. Free shipping at $35. The price reflects the cost of doing it right — not the cost of looking expensive.

Founder

Ashley March,
Bonney Lake, WA.

Ashley founded NoodleBomb in 2024 in her own kitchen. She still tastes every batch. Every label gets her signature on the back.
