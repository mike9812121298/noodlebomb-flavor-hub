What to buy

The spicy sauce for ramen nights, rice bowls, and fast weeknight food.

Spicy Tokyo is the NoodleBomb Pour bottle for people who want heat without losing the savory base. It is built for ramen first: stir it into broth, toss it with noodles, or use it as the flavor anchor when instant ramen needs more body.

The same bottle also works when the meal is not ramen. Use it on rice bowls, fried rice, wings, eggs, vegetables, stir fry, and quick marinades. Start with a tablespoon, taste, then turn the volume up.

  • Use Spicy Tokyo when you want chili-garlic heat and deep umami.
  • Use Original when you want bold flavor without extra heat.
  • Use Citrus Shoyu when you want a brighter ramen bowl or rice bowl.
  • Use the Trio when you want all three Pour flavors in one $29.99 pack that meets the automatic free US shipping threshold.
How to use it

One bottle. More than one bowl.

Ramen

Add 1 tablespoon to a bowl, stir in hot broth or hot water, then add noodles and toppings. Add more if you want a bolder bowl.

Noodles

Toss cooked noodles with Spicy Tokyo, a splash of hot noodle water, scallions, and sesame for a fast lunch or dinner.

Wings

Use Spicy Tokyo as the flavor base for crispy wings. Toss while hot, then finish with lime or scallions if that is your move.
